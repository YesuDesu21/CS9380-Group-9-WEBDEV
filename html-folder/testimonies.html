GENDER BASED VIOLENCE AND EQUALITY

STATISTICS

1 in 3 women worldwide experience physical or sexual violence in their lifetime (World Health Organization [WHO], 2021).

Women make up 70% of health and social workers globally but earn on average 28% less than men in the same sector (UN Women, 2022).

LGBTQ+ individuals face higher rates of workplace discrimination; in the U.S., 46% of LGBTQ+ workers reported hiding their identity at work to avoid bias (Human Rights Campaign [HRC], 2022).

In the Philippines, 1 in 4 women aged 15–49 has experienced physical, emotional, or sexual violence from their partner (Philippine (Statistics Authority [PSA], 2017).

TESTIMONIES

Erna Surjadi, Gender Advocate

“I spent over 15 years in the gender movement, gender mainstreaming in multisectors; however, nowadays I see more discrimination, injustice to women, especially due to the financial crisis and economic situation. Gender equality will benefit the family because women are the centre of the family and play the most important role in the future of the family, community, and nation.”

Manu Gambagorte, Global Creative Strategy Leader (former Meta, Spotify, Google)

“I started my career 16 years ago, although I had some great achievements. There were always some ‘banana peels’ in the way. Small, sometimes hard-to-spot biases were constantly there. Making me question my worth and feel confused, distressed, drained, and sometimes insomniac. I’ve been told not to go to the meeting. because there were already ‘too many people in the room.’ Then I was told to go to ‘beautify’ the room. I was told I should go to the meeting but stay quiet. I’ve been denied. I want to submit my project for an industry award and move away from a beer account. I was the only female and foreigner because there was no ‘cultural fit’. I had to ‘reinvent the industry’. At the same time, male peers were getting promoted just by doing their jobs. All these stories show that there is still much to fight for.”

Deborah, Aspiring Bus Driver (Victoria, Australia)

“When I asked him to explain his comments. Except that I was a woman and driving a bus was, in his eyes, ‘man’s work’. He insisted I’d have to stay on the lower training wage until I’d proven myself.” She filed a formal complaint, and as a result, the company apologized, committed to equal-opportunity training, and ensured she received appropriate pay.

These voices illustrate the personal struggles that statistics cannot fully capture. They show that inequality manifests differently across cultures, professions, and identities — but the common thread is that it limits human dignity and opportunity.

SOLUTIONS & POSITIVE
EXPERIENCES

Safe Reporting Systems: Workplaces that implement anonymous reporting tools for harassment and discrimination see up to 30% higher incident reporting and greater accountability.

Empowerment Programs: Grassroots initiatives, such as women-led cooperatives in Southeast Asia, have lifted thousands out of poverty by providing financial independence.

Role Models: Testimonies from women leaders, such as CEOs, scientists, and politicians, inspire younger generations to pursue paths once seen as “off-limits.”

Community Engagement: Men who join campaigns (e.g., UN’s HeForShe movement) report stronger family and workplace relationships, showing that equality benefits everyone.
