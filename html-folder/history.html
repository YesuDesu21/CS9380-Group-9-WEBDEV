TRACING THE STRUGGLE FOR GENDER EQUALITY

A Timeline of Key Events and Milestones in Gender Equality

THE HISTORY

The goal of gender equality is to guarantee that all people, regardless of gender, have the same rights and opportunities. It places a strong emphasis on taking down obstacles that stand in the way of people realising their greatest potential. Due to disparate social, cultural, and political structures, prejudice against women and gender minorities has existed for a long time. Legal changes as well as cultural changes that dispel prejudices and antiquated ideas are necessary to achieve real equality.


Promoting gender equality is advantageous to society at large. Equal access to healthcare, education, employment, and politics fosters diversity, creativity, and prosperity in communities. By enabling everyone to reach their greatest potential, it also promotes economic progress. Gender biases must be addressed and eradicated in order to create a setting where everyone may prosper.

TIMELINE

  • 1832

    A petition is presented to a Member of Parliament advocating for unmarried women to be granted the right to vote.

  • 1848

    The first women's suffrage conference convenes in Seneca Falls, New York, calling for equal voting rights for women.

  • 1893

    New Zealand becomes the first country to grant women the right to vote.

  • 1897

    The National Union of Women's Suffrage Societies is established in the UK to campaign for women's suffrage.

  • 1903

    The Women's Social and Political Union is founded in the UK, intensifying the suffrage movement.

  • 1916

    Women in Manitoba, Canada, become the first to vote in a provincial election.

  • 1918

    Some women in the UK are granted the right to vote.

  • 1920

    The 19th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution is ratified, granting women the right to vote.

  • 1923

    The National Woman's Party proposes the Equal Rights Amendment in the U.S. Congress.

  • 1929

    The "Persons Case" in Canada affirms that women are "persons" under the law, allowing them to serve in the Senate.

  • 1948

    The Universal Declaration of Human Rights is adopted by the UN General Assembly, including gender equality as a fundamental human right.

  • 1962

    The first Moroccan constitution grants equal political rights to men and women, allowing Moroccan women to vote and run for election.

  • 1970

    An international feminist movement gains momentum, advocating for women's rights and gender equality.

  • 1970

    The Women's Strike for Equality takes place in the U.S., demanding equal opportunities in employment and education.

  • 1973

    The U.S. Supreme Court's Roe v. Wade decision legalizes abortion, affirming women's reproductive rights.

  • 1975

    The UN General Assembly declares the year as International Women's Year and organizes the first World Conference on Women in Mexico City.

  • 1981

    The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW) is adopted by the UN General Assembly.

  • 1995

    The Fourth World Conference on Women is held in Beijing, resulting in the Beijing Declaration and Platform for Action.

  • 2000

    The UN Security Council adopts Resolution 1325, recognizing the impact of war on women and calling for their participation in peace processes.

  • 2005

    The UN adopts the Millennium Development Goals, with Goal 3 focusing on promoting gender equality and empowering women.

  • 2010

    The UN Women is established to advocate for gender equality and the empowerment of women.

  • 2015

    The UN adopts the Sustainable Development Goals, with Goal 5 aiming to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.

  • 2015

    The term Toxic Masculinity was mentioned a lot and was being discussed scholarly.

  • 2020

    The theme "I am Generation Equality: Realizing Women’s Rights" was launched, emphasizing the need for collective action to achieve gender equality.

  • 2022

    Voices of both men and women were heard and the International Men’s Day was declared by Gender Equality Agency of Bosnia and Herzegovina event

  • 2025

    The UN released the Gender Snapshot 2025, highlighting progress and challenges in gender equality, including the need for renewed commitments to achieve Sustainable Development Goal
